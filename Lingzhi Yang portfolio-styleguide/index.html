About Me

Lingzhi Yang is a digital artist from Shanghai, currently studying at Simon Fraser University's School of Interactive Arts and Technology (SIAT).

With a profound passion for storytelling and visual innovation, Lingzhi brings ideas to life through a diverse skill set in Adobe Photoshop, After Effects, and Premiere Pro.

His work effortlessly combines artistic creativity and technical expertise, resulting in visually stunning animations, professional-grade video edits, and captivating image designs.

In addition to visual media, Lingzhi is experienced in 3D modeling and design, using tools such as Onshape and Rhino to explore form, structure, and spatial thinking.

He also designs user interfaces in Figma and brings them to life using HTML and CSS, enabling him to build clean, functional, and visually consistent web experiences.

Furthermore, Lingzhi's proficiency in Java and Python empowers him to integrate cutting-edge technology seamlessly into his projects.

Driven by endless curiosity and a relentless pursuit of creativity, Lingzhi continuously explores new ways to blend art and technology, crafting digital experiences that resonate deeply with audiences.

Project 01

Cyber City Farm

Java Game Design Interactive Media

Cyber City Farm is an interactive Java game that combines farming simulation with action-based gameplay. In this project, the player takes care of a rabbit by planting seeds, growing carrots, harvesting crops, and feeding the rabbit, while also defending it from attacking robots. The game includes multiple states, character interactions, sound effects, animation, and a survival timer to create a more engaging experience.

My Role

I designed and developed the full game system, including gameplay logic, character behavior, animation handling, collision detection, and visual/audio integration.

Tools

Java, object-oriented programming, custom game logic, image assets, sound effects, and interactive state design.

Development Steps

I first planned the basic game structure and separated the project into different gameplay states such as the intro screen, active gameplay, and game over screen. After that, I built the core farming system so the player could plant seeds and harvest carrots. I then added the rabbit character as the center of the game and designed interactions where the rabbit could eat carrots and react to the player's actions.

In the next stage, I introduced robot enemies to add pressure and challenge. This required me to design movement, collisions, and a shooting system using carrot bullets. I also added sound effects, background music, and simple animations to make the experience feel more alive and responsive. Finally, I refined the timing system, balanced the gameplay flow, and organized the code into different classes so the project would be easier to manage and expand.

Outcome

This project helped me practice combining creative game ideas with technical problem-solving. It also strengthened my understanding of interactive systems, class structure, and how visual and audio elements can improve player experience.

Project 02

Transformers Text Animation

After Effects Motion Design Typography

This project is a dynamic text animation inspired by Optimus Prime's iconic lines from Transformers. I used Adobe After Effects as the main tool and explored motion, pacing, and visual rhythm through animated typography. Additional tools such as Premiere Pro, Photoshop, PowerPoint, and Meitu supported the editing and visual development process.

My Role

I developed the full concept, visual direction, text animation, motion timing, and final video editing for this project.

Tools

Adobe After Effects, Premiere Pro, Photoshop, PowerPoint, and Meitu.

Design and Development Process

I began by choosing a well-known Transformers monologue and using it as the foundation for the animation. My goal was to create a video that felt dramatic, cinematic, and visually powerful through text alone. In the early stage, I focused on writing and organizing the line script so I could break the speech into sections with different pacing and emphasis.

After that, I started experimenting in After Effects with text movement, scale, rhythm, and transitions. My first version felt too simple, so I researched more advanced motion design techniques and tested different ways to create stronger visual impact. I explored methods such as camera movement, layered typography, and timing adjustments to make the animation feel more dynamic and expressive.

In the final stage, I refined the composition, improved readability, and adjusted the overall pacing of the video. I also used additional tools to support editing and visual enhancement. This process helped me better understand how motion, typography, and timing can work together to create a stronger emotional effect.

Outcome

This project strengthened my skills in motion graphics and visual storytelling. It also helped me learn how to build mood and intensity through animated text instead of relying only on images or characters.
